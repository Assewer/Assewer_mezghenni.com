Financial Market Project

In this academic financial market project, we directly interacted with brokers, negotiating and securing a highly competitive 2% brokerage fee.

We employed Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT) for optimal diversification, aiming to minimize risk while maximizing returns by constructing an efficient frontier. Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) was used to evaluate the expected return on individual securities relative to their systemic risk (beta).

During the trading process, we performed detailed fundamental analysis to assess intrinsic company values and macroeconomic trends, examining financial ratios like P/E ratio, ROE, and EBITDA, to assess company health and market potential. For market timing and pattern recognition, technical analysis was applied using MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) and many other tools.
